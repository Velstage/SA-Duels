[SA Duel 3D Model Art Style Invariant]

Overall Style:
- Semi-realistic 3D fighting game character model style.
- Looks like an in-game 3D model or promotional render from a console fighting game.
- Not photorealistic.
- Not anime illustration.
- Not painterly concept art.
- Stylized but grounded 3D character rendering.
- Slightly simplified detail level compared to modern hyper-realistic AAA games.
- Clean, readable silhouettes designed for fighting game character recognition.

Rendering Quality:
- Medium-high quality 3D render.
- Smooth but not overly detailed skin shading.
- Subtle plastic-like or polished game-model surface quality is acceptable.
- Avoid excessive pores, wrinkles, micro skin texture, or realistic imperfections.
- Avoid cinematic ultra-realism.
- Avoid extremely high-resolution fashion-photo lighting.
- Details should look like they belong to a playable game character model.

Character Proportions:
- Attractive stylized human proportions.
- Slightly idealized body shapes.
- Fighting game proportions: athletic, readable, slightly exaggerated.
- Faces are clean and symmetrical, but not hyper-realistic.
- Eyes may be slightly larger and clearer than real life, while still fitting a 3D semi-real style.
- Body silhouette should be clear even from a distance.

Face Style:
- Smooth 3D-rendered face.
- Soft facial features with simplified planes.
- Clear eyes, defined nose, and clean lips.
- Minimal skin texture.
- Expression should be readable but not overly dramatic.
- Avoid heavy realism, uncanny valley, or photo-like facial detail.
- Avoid anime flat-face style.
- Face should feel like a fighting game roster character portrait.

Hair Style:
- Hair should be modeled as stylized 3D hair strands or grouped locks.
- Hair has volume and shape, but individual strands are not overly realistic.
- Glossy highlights are acceptable.
- Hair should look like a game model asset, not real hair simulation.
- Avoid messy micro-strands or hyper-detailed hair physics.
- Maintain a clean, sculpted silhouette.

Costume / Material Style:
- Costumes should have clean material separation.
- Leather, fabric, metal, armor, gloves, and accessories should be readable through simple but clear shading.
- Materials may have glossy highlights, especially leather or bodysuit surfaces.
- Patterns and decorative details should be visible but not overly intricate.
- Avoid photorealistic fabric weave or excessive tiny ornamentation.
- Costume detail level should support character identity without clutter.

Lighting:
- Bright, direct game-stage lighting.
- Clear rim light or highlight is allowed.
- Lighting should make the character readable.
- Avoid overly dark cinematic lighting where details disappear.
- Avoid moody realistic film lighting unless specifically requested.
- Stage-like lighting is preferred: clean, strong, and slightly artificial.

Color:
- Saturated but controlled colors.
- Character colors should be clear and recognizable.
- Skin tones are smooth and clean.
- Costumes may use bold color blocking.
- Avoid muddy colors.
- Avoid overly realistic desaturated palettes unless the scene requires it.

Camera / Composition:
- Fighting game camera logic.
- Characters should be posed clearly, usually facing the camera or opponent.
- Full-body fighting stance, mid-action side view, or roster-poster composition are preferred.
- Poses should emphasize silhouette, movement, and combat readability.
- Avoid extreme cinematic angles that hide the character design.
- Avoid overly close fashion portrait framing unless specifically requested.

Background / Stage:
- Backgrounds can be simplified 3D fighting game stages.
- Arena, city, temple, stadium, rooftop, ruins, or stage-like environments are acceptable.
- Background detail should be lower priority than the characters.
- Slight depth blur is acceptable.
- Background should not overpower the character.
- UI-like fighting game composition is allowed when needed.

Action Effects:
- Motion blur, impact flashes, dust, sparks, smoke, and light trails are allowed.
- Effects should feel like fighting game visual effects.
- Effects should not obscure the character’s key features too much.
- Avoid excessive particles that create messy noise.
- Avoid painterly explosion effects unless matching a promotional poster scene.

Texture Detail Level:
- Moderate detail.
- Smooth surfaces.
- Clear but simplified clothing seams and armor edges.
- No hyper-detailed pores, fabric fibers, scratches, dirt, or battle damage unless specifically requested.
- The model should remain clean and readable.

Do:
- Make it look like a 3D fighting game model.
- Keep character identity readable.
- Use clean silhouettes.
- Use simple but attractive material shading.
- Use strong game-like lighting.
- Keep the render slightly stylized and slightly artificial.

Do Not:
- Do not make it photorealistic.
- Do not make it anime 2D.
- Do not make it painterly.
- Do not add excessive realistic skin texture.
- Do not over-detail clothing or accessories.
- Do not use dark cinematic lighting that hides the design.
- Do not make the character look like a live-action actor.
- Do not make it look like a modern ultra-realistic Unreal Engine tech demo.
- Do not use gritty realism unless requested.
